# People that are non a student are more than people that are student
plot1 = ggplot(data=df,mapping=aes(x=Gender,y=Balance)) + geom_boxplot(color='black',fill='yellow')
plot2 = ggplot(data=df,mapping=aes(x=Married,y=Balance)) + geom_boxplot(color='black',fill='yellow')
plot3 = ggplot(data=df,mapping=aes(x=Student,y=Balance)) + geom_boxplot(color='black',fill='yellow')
grid.arrange(plot1,plot2,plot3,ncol=3)

Credit balance for male and female has no significant difference. Credit balance for married or not married has no significant difference. This shows that gender and marital status has no linear correlation with the credit balance. The credit balance for students is higher on average than those that are not student. However, the sample size for student is too small to draw a confident conclusion.
